Blocking everything is not a life.

You keep adding one more site, one more app, one more rule, and the computer slowly turns into a cage. Explicit content still slips through the cracks. Brake is built for those cracks.

Where it still gets through
01

Search results

Image grids and previews can expose content before a blocker catches the site.

02

Social feeds

Reddit, X, forums, and feeds can surface explicit content through posts, thumbnails, and links.

03

In-app browsers

Apps can open web views that normal browser rules do not always cover cleanly.

04

Game and app overlays

Steam overlays and embedded browsers can create unexpected access points.

05

Random links

One click from a safe page can land somewhere risky.

06

Sites you forgot

A blacklist is only as complete as the last rule you remembered to add.

Brake watches what actually appears on screen.

No list to maintain. Brake watches what is on your screen and reacts the moment explicit content appears.

Detection
On-device AI detection
A local model reads your screen in near real time. Screenshots never leave the machine.
Response
A lockout with real weight
Explicit content locks the computer for a timer you set, and can escalate to a shutdown.
Performance
Light on your computer
Tuned to be very light on the CPU, so your machine keeps feeling normal while it runs.
Privacy

It works entirely on your computer.

Detection runs as a local model. Nothing you do is uploaded, recorded, or sent anywhere. No account, no sync, no server.
